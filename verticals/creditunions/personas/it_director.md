# Persona: IT Director — Credit Unions

## Role and Scope
IT Directors at credit unions own technology infrastructure, integration decisions, and digital member experience platforms. They have significant influence on technology purchases and must ensure all solutions meet compliance requirements while integrating seamlessly with core banking systems.

## Relationship to Heymarket
Primary buyer and technical evaluator. Key questions focus on security, compliance, and integration capabilities. Won by demonstrating seamless core banking integration, robust security features, and measurable member experience improvements.

## Day-to-Day Pressures
• Ensuring all member communications meet strict financial compliance requirements
• Managing integration complexity with legacy core banking systems
• Reducing IT support burden while improving member digital experience
• Balancing security requirements with user-friendly member communication
• Justifying technology investments with measurable ROI and member satisfaction gains
• Supporting multiple departments (lending, collections, member services) with unified solutions
• Maintaining system uptime and reliability for critical member communications

## Key Objections and Responses
• "Security concerns with member data" → Enterprise-grade encryption and financial services compliance certifications
• "Integration complexity with our core system" → Pre-built integrations and dedicated implementation support
• "Budget constraints and ROI uncertainty" → Demonstrable cost savings through call deflection and efficiency gains
• "Compliance and regulatory requirements" → Built-in audit trails and financial services compliance features

## Language They Use
Core banking platform, member experience, digital transformation, API integration, data security, compliance audit, system integration, member portal, CRM integration, workflow automation, NCUA regulations, member engagement metrics, call deflection, operational efficiency

## What Makes Them Reply to a Cold Email
• Specific integration capabilities with their core banking system
• Quantifiable operational efficiency improvements and cost savings
• Security and compliance certifications relevant to financial services
• Success stories from similar-sized credit unions with measurable results

Delete triggers:
• Generic sales pitches without industry context
• Lack of understanding of financial services compliance requirements
• Pushy follow-ups without value-add content
• Solutions that seem to require major system overhauls

## Best Heymarket Proof Points for This Persona
• Shared inbox visibility provides complete audit trail for compliance requirements
• Seamless CRM integration maintains centralized member communication history
• Enterprise administration features enable proper user management and security controls
• Unified communication platform reduces IT complexity versus managing multiple point solutions
• Automated workflows reduce manual processes while maintaining compliance oversight
• Proven integration capabilities with existing help desk and CRM systems

## Sequence Arc
T1–T2: Core banking integration and operational efficiency focus
T3–T4: Security, compliance, and audit trail capabilities
T5–T6: Peer success stories and quantifiable ROI from similar credit unions
T7–T8: Technical deep-dive and implementation support differentiation
