
## Touch 1 — Cold intro
**LEARNINGS FOR NEXT RUN:**

1. **Hook structures**: Direct pain statements scored highest (hook=8) vs questions (hook=6-7). "When examiners audit..." and "Noticed most credit union staff..." outperformed "Most credit union marketing gets ignored." Lead with specific, observable IT Director pain.

2. **Proof patterns**: All variants scored proof=5-7 because non-financial industry examples (U-Haul, fitness, healthcare) feel irrelevant and templated to credit union IT Directors. Need financial services proof or make the connection more credible.

3. **CTA patterns**: Direct questions about their current state consistently scored cta=8. "How do you currently track..." and "What's your biggest challenge with..." work better than "Which tools are you using..."

4. **Tone patterns**: Scored best (tone=7-8) when avoiding sales-speak. "Bet you manage separate contracts" and compliance language resonated. Avoid "we helped" and "result was" phrasing.

5. **Avoid**: Question openers for hooks, unrelated industry proof points, productivity/efficiency angles (scored relevance=7 vs compliance at relevance=9), and generic "we helped a company" proof structure.

## Touch 2 — Follow-up
## Learnings for Next Iteration

1. **Hook Structure**: Direct statement openers ("Bet {{company}} runs...") scored highest (hook=8), outperforming question openers (hook=6-7). Lead with confident assertions about their current state rather than questions.

2. **Proof Patterns**: Technical specificity wins - "Salesforce integration" scored higher (proof=7) than vague outcomes like "20% better response rates" (proof=5-6). IT Directors need concrete technical details, not marketing metrics.

3. **CTA Success**: Direct questions about current tool count ("How many platforms...") consistently scored cta=8, while preference questions ("Which tool do your staff resist most...") dropped to cta=7.

4. **Tone Optimization**: Variant B's confident, technical language scored tone=8, while empathy-heavy approaches ("staff drowning") scored tone=6. IT Directors prefer assertive problem identification over emotional appeals.

5. **Avoid**: Healthcare/fitness proof points consistently underperform (proof=5-6). Stop using vague testimonial quotes. Question openers and emotional language ("drowning," "chaos") consistently score lower across all criteria.

## Touch 3 — Value add
**LEARNINGS FOR NEXT ITERATION:**

1. **Hook structures**: Vendor consolidation pain point opener scored hook=7, member experience consistency opener scored hook=6. Direct problem statements outperformed abstract concepts. Test specific pain points (security, compliance, integration complexity).

2. **Proof weakness**: Both variants scored proof=6 using same generic U-Haul case study without metrics. Need specific numbers (% vendor reduction, exact cost savings, measurable outcomes) and ideally credit union case studies, not generic examples.

3. **CTA patterns**: Direct operational questions scored best - "How many platforms does {{company}} pay for?" (cta=8) vs abstract experience questions (cta=6). Ask about countable/measurable current state.

4. **Tone patterns**: Both scored tone=6 - professional but not engaging enough. Need more conversational, less corporate language while maintaining IT credibility.

5. **Avoid**: Generic case studies without numbers, abstract member experience angles over concrete operational pain, questions about subjective experiences vs measurable business metrics.
