# ICP Profile — Retail Vertical

Source: Q4 2024 ICP project, 7,700 closed/won opportunities, Clearbit-enriched.

---

## Overview

Retail is Heymarket's highest average MRR vertical at 19% of total revenue. It is the primary build target for sequences, persona profiles, and optimization runs.

## Company Size

| Dimension     | Sweet Spot            | Full Range           |
|---------------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Employees     | 1,000–5,000           | 51–50,000            |
| Revenue       | $250M–$500M           | $1M–$10B             |
| Segment       | Midmarket (primary)   | SMB to Enterprise    |

Sweet spot is midmarket: enough complexity to need shared inboxes and team workflows, not so large that procurement cycles become multi-quarter.

---

## Tech Stack Priority

| Stack                        | Priority | Signal                                      |
|------------------------------|----------|---------------------------------------------|
| HubSpot only                 | P1       | Higher MRR, shorter deal cycle              |
| Salesforce only              | P2       | Strong fit, longer cycle                    |
| HubSpot + Salesforce combo   | Highest  | Highest MRR, enterprise-tier deal size      |

Detection method: Clay enrichment on inbound and outbound lists. HubSpot presence is the strongest single predictor of deal velocity in retail.

## Use Cases — Top 100 Customers

| Use Case                    | Frequency | Avg Deal Size Weight | Notes                                     |
|-----------------------------|-----------|----------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Customer service/engagement | 64.8%     | 25.2%                | Primary entry point — use for T1          |
| Recruiting/staffing/HR      | 15.5%     | 37.4%                | Highest deal size — strong expansion path |
| Operations                  | 12.7%     | 18.9%                | Use for T5 (secondary angle)              |
| Marketing/outbound          | 7.0%      | 18.5%                | Weakest entry, strongest expansion        |

Customer service and engagement is the most common entry use case and should anchor T1 openers. Recruiting/HR has the highest deal size and is a strong upsell path post-close — do not lead with it cold.

## Competitors in This Vertical

- **Braze** — enterprise push/email/SMS, one-way broadcast, heavy implementation
- **Klaviyo** — ecommerce email/SMS marketing, one-way blast, no shared inbox
- **Attentive** — SMS marketing, broadcast-focused, no team inbox or routing
- **Brevo** (formerly Sendinblue) — email/SMS marketing, limited two-way capability

Displacement angle: prospects using any of the above are paying for broadcast-only. The gap is receiving replies. Use this in T1 only when signals confirm they're running one of these tools.

---

## Exclusion List

Do not target these industries even if they appear in retail-adjacent databases:
- Education
- Real Estate
- Software / SaaS
- Government / Public Sector

---

## Qualification Signals (prioritize outreach when present)

- HubSpot or Salesforce detected in tech stack (Clay, BuiltWith)
- 500+ employees in retail sub-industry
- Job postings for Customer Success, Store Operations, or IT/Systems roles
- Recent hiring spike in CS or Ops (indicates growth, comms strain)
- Company uses Klaviyo or Attentive (two-way gap displacement opportunity)
