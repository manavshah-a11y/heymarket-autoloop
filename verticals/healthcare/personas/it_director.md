# Persona: IT Director — Healthcare

## Role and Scope
IT Directors in healthcare organizations own technology infrastructure, system integrations, and digital transformation initiatives. They have significant influence on software purchasing decisions and must ensure all solutions meet HIPAA compliance and integrate seamlessly with existing EHR systems.

## Relationship to Heymarket
Primary buyer and implementation owner, though not daily user. Key questions center on security, compliance, integration complexity, and system reliability. Won by demonstrating enterprise-grade security, seamless EHR integration, and proven healthcare customer success.

## Day-to-Day Pressures
- Ensuring HIPAA compliance across all patient communication channels
- Managing complex integrations between EHR, CRM, and communication platforms
- Reducing IT tickets and support requests from staff struggling with fragmented systems
- Balancing security requirements with staff productivity and patient experience
- Justifying ROI on technology investments to C-suite and board members
- Maintaining system uptime and reliability for patient-critical communications
- Supporting staff adoption of new technologies while minimizing training overhead

## Key Objections and Responses
**"How do you ensure HIPAA compliance?"** — Enterprise-grade encryption, BAA included, SOC 2 certified
**"Will this integrate with our Epic system?"** — Native EHR integrations with proven healthcare customers
**"We already have communication tools."** — Unified platform eliminates silos, reduces vendor complexity
**"Implementation seems complex."** — Dedicated healthcare onboarding team, proven 30-day rollouts

## Language They Use
HIPAA, BAA, PHI, EHR integration, Epic, Cerner, patient workflow, care coordination, operational efficiency, system interoperability, API, SSO, two-factor authentication, audit trails, data governance, patient engagement platform

## What Makes Them Reply to a Cold Email
- References to specific EHR integration capabilities
- Healthcare compliance and security certifications
- ROI data from similar-sized healthcare organizations  
- Mention of reducing IT overhead and support tickets

What makes them delete:
- Generic "business texting" without healthcare context
- No mention of compliance or security
- Salesy language without technical substance
- Unclear integration capabilities with existing systems

## Best Heymarket Proof Points for This Persona
- HIPAA-compliant platform with included Business Associate Agreement
- Native integrations with Epic, Salesforce, and HubSpot used by healthcare customers
- Shared inbox visibility reduces IT support requests for communication issues
- Enterprise security features including audit trails and user permissions
- Healthcare customer testimonials showing 25% response rate improvements
- Proven implementation process with healthcare-specific onboarding team

T1–T2: HIPAA compliance and security positioning
T3–T4: EHR integration capabilities and technical proof points  
T5–T6: Healthcare customer success stories and ROI data
